Subject: Monetization, and how it ties to the role-at-signup question

Hi Jake,

On monetization, here's where the system sits today and the handful of decisions
that drive it.

HOW IT'S BILLED NOW

It's a per-seat subscription. Only "staff" (Admin, Team Lead, Agent, Transaction
Coordinator, Attorney) consume a paid seat. The Solo plan covers one seat (the
founder); the Team plan adds teammates up to a seat limit. Clients, FSBO
sellers, and vendors are free under this model because they're brought in by a
paying customer and only see their own deal.

WHY IT COUPLES WITH THE ROLE QUESTION

Today the only person who pays is whoever creates the workspace. Everyone else is
either a paid teammate (invited) or a free portal user. So a stranger
self-signing up as "Title Vendor" has no workspace to live in and nobody paying
for them, which is the same gap we hit on the role-at-signup question.

THE DECISIONS I'D WANT FROM YOU

- Who is the paying customer: the solo agent, or the brokerage? That sets
  whether we lead with single seats or team plans.

- Do vendors ever become a revenue line (paid directory or lead-gen), or stay
  free forever?

- Pricing shape: flat per-seat per month, or tied to transaction volume?

Once we settle those, the signup and role flow basically designs itself. Send
me your take on those three and I'll lock it down.
