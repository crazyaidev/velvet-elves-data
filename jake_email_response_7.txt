Subject: How accounts, organizations, and teams work today

Hi Jake,

Here's a simple picture of how the system currently handles organizations and
team management. I'll keep it in plain terms.


1. EVERY ACCOUNT STARTS ITS OWN ORGANIZATION

When someone signs up, the system creates a brand-new, private workspace (think
of it as their own brokerage) and makes that person the Admin and owner of it.
Each organization is sealed off from every other one, so no account can ever see
another's deals, documents, or contacts. That's the core reason a fresh signup
defaults to Admin: they're the first and only person in a workspace they own.


2. TWO KINDS OF PEOPLE LIVE INSIDE AN ORGANIZATION

- Staff (the people who work the deals): Admin, Team Lead, Agent, Transaction
  Coordinator, and Attorney. Staff take up a paid seat.

- Portal users (limited, deal-specific access): Clients, For Sale By Owner
  sellers, and Vendors (title, mortgage, inspection, and so on). They are always
  free and never use a paid seat.


3. TWO WAYS TO BRING SOMEONE INTO YOUR ORGANIZATION

- Invite them: an existing member sends a one-time email invitation. The person
  clicks it, sets their own password, and lands in your workspace with the role
  you chose.

- Add them to a deal: when an agent adds a client or vendor to a transaction,
  the system automatically assigns that person the right role and emails them a
  welcome and login link. They do not pick or change their own role.


4. YOU CAN ONLY INVITE AT OR BELOW YOUR OWN LEVEL

This keeps the chain of authority clean:

- Admin can invite anyone.
- Team Lead can invite Team Leads, Coordinators, Agents, Attorneys, and any
  portal user.
- Transaction Coordinator can invite Agents, Attorneys, and portal users.
- Agent can invite other Agents and portal users.

Nobody can create someone above themselves, and nobody can change their own
role. Role changes are made by an Admin in the team settings, and the owner is
protected so they can't be accidentally demoted or locked out.


5. TEAMS ARE SUB-GROUPS INSIDE ONE ORGANIZATION

A single organization can be split into Teams, each with its own Team Lead.
Agents and Coordinators can be assigned to a team so leads manage their own
people and deals. The Admin oversees all teams; a Team Lead manages just theirs.


6. PLANS AND SEATS

- Solo plan: just the founder, no extra staff.
- Team plan: invite teammates up to your seat limit.
- Clients and vendors are always free, no matter the plan.


THE SHORT VERSION

Each signup owns a private brokerage. Staff are paid seats and work the deals;
clients and vendors are free and only see their own transaction. People join
either by invitation or by being added to a deal, always at a role their inviter
is allowed to grant, and only an Admin can change someone's role afterward.

Happy to walk through any of this or adjust how it should work for MVP.
