KEY DEPENDENCIES & RISKS
================================================================================

1. Task Spreadsheet Delivery: Client must provide REWORKING TASK DB.xlsx
   by Week 2 for task catalogue import.

2. API Credentials: Gmail, Outlook, DocuSign, Stripe, and OpenAI API
   credentials/accounts must be available by Week 9 (AI), Week 13 (e-sign),
   Week 14 (email), Week 18 (payments).

3. Legacy Codebase Access: Access to current ASP.NET MVC codebase needed
   by Week 1 for review.

4. Client Feedback Loops: Wizard flow (Week 11) requires client review and
   approval within 2-3 business days to avoid timeline slippage.
   NOTE: Active Transactions page design approved by client (2026-03-19) and
   moved into Week 8 implementation. Dashboard landing pages for Solo Agent,
   Team Leader, Attorney, and FSBO approved by client (2026-03-26) and
   incorporated into Milestone 5.1 deliverables. Active Transactions UI
   alignment (key dates, modals, transaction history, AI chat) finalized
   2026-03-30 and incorporated into Milestones 2.1 and 2.4.

5. Third-Party API Limitations: MLS feed access, e-signature provider
   sandbox availability, and email provider OAuth approval processes
   may introduce delays.

6. Scope Creep: The advertising module, consumer-facing site, and some
   advanced AI features (NLP task creation, predictive analytics) are
   scoped minimally for MVP. Expanding these risks timeline overrun.

7. AI Coach Feature: The AI Coach add-on shown in Team Leader design files
   is a future paid feature ($79/agent/month) and NOT part of MVP. MVP may
   preserve architecture hooks or feature-flagged placeholders but should not
   scope active implementation into the core delivery.

8. Attorney AI Guardrails: Attorney dashboard implementation must enforce
   explicit boundaries between AI-prepared work and human legal judgment.
   Final legal position, packet release approval, and same-day disbursement
   exceptions remain human-owned. Testing must verify these guardrails.
