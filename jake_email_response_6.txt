THE TWO QUICK FIXES
-------------------

1. Phone number dropping a digit / adding the "1."
   You're right, and it's a small bug. The field only keeps 10 digits. When a
   "1" country code slips in (usually from your phone/browser autofill), the
   system counts that "1" as the first digit and chops the last real digit off
   the end. I'll strip the leading country-code "1" so it always keeps your
   true 10-digit number, exactly as you asked.

2. Company / Brokerage not carrying over.
   Also a real gap. What you typed in "Company / Brokerage" at sign-up IS being
   saved, but it's being used behind the scenes to NAME your new organization,
   and it was never copied into the "Company / Brokerage" box on Step 2. So that
   box looks empty even though the info wasn't lost. I'll pre-fill Step 2 with
   what you entered.


WHY IT SHOWS "ADMIN," AND WHY THE ROLE IS LOCKED ON STEP 2
----------------------------------------------------------

This part is by design, not a glitch. Today the system assumes that anyone who
signs up on the public page is the owner of a brand-new workspace, so it
automatically makes them the ADMIN of their own organization. The role is shown
as read-only on Step 2 on purpose: if someone could change their own role during
setup, they could accidentally demote themselves out of being the owner and lock
themselves out of their own account. Role changes are meant to be made by an
Admin from the team settings, not self-served during onboarding.

So nothing is broken here, but it doesn't match what you want, which brings us
to the real question below.


VENDORS & CLIENTS VIA TRANSACTIONS: ALREADY WORKS THE WAY YOU DESCRIBED
----------------------------------------------------------------------

Your understanding is correct and already live: when an agent adds a vendor or
client to a transaction, the system AUTOMATICALLY assigns them that role and
sends them a welcome/invite email when the transaction is set up. Those users
CANNOT change their own role, exactly the behavior you described. One note: the
system uses a single "Vendor" role with a CATEGORY on top (Title, Mortgage,
Inspection, etc.), so "Title Vendor" and "Mortgage Vendor" are captured as
Vendor + category. That's already in place.


THE ONE THING WE SHOULD ALIGN ON: LETTING PEOPLE PICK A ROLE AT SIGN-UP
-----------------------------------------------------------------------

What you're asking for, a dropdown at sign-up (Agent, Team Leader, Attorney,
FSBO, Transaction Coordinator, Mortgage Vendor, Title Vendor) AND the ability to
change it on Step 2, is a change to that "every sign-up owns a new workspace"
assumption above. It's very doable, but it raises one question worth deciding
before I build it:

   If a brand-new person signs up and picks "Title Vendor" (or Agent, Attorney,
   etc.), WHICH ORGANIZATION do they belong to? In the current design,
   vendors/agents/clients live INSIDE an existing brokerage's workspace; they're
   invited in or added to a deal. A self-signup with no organization yet has
   nowhere to "be a Title Vendor."

That's the same thread as your "we'll set up the Brokerage manually later, not
for MVP" note. The cleanest MVP path is probably: keep self-signup creating the
person's own workspace, but LET THEM SELF-LABEL their role (and make it editable
on Step 2, with the founder/owner protected so they can't lock themselves out).
That gets you the dropdown and the "people are stupid, let them fix it" safety
net without having to solve brokerage hierarchy first.
