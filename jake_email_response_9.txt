Subject: Changing roles, joining teams, and connecting agents with a TC

Hi Jake,

Answers to your three questions, grounded in how the system works today.


A. HOW DOES SOMEONE CHANGE THEIR ACCOUNT TYPE (e.g., Team Leader vs Admin)?

An Admin in your brokerage changes it. In Team settings, the Admin opens the
member and switches their role (Agent, Team Lead, Transaction Coordinator,
Attorney, or Admin). People can't change their own role, which stops anyone from
accidentally locking themselves out or promoting themselves. Two guardrails sit
on top: the brokerage owner's role is locked (ownership has to be transferred
before their role can change), and moving someone into a paid staff role checks
that you have an open seat on your plan.

So "become a Team Leader" means an Admin promotes that person to Team Lead. That
is the only path today.


B. HOW DOES SOMEONE JOIN A TEAM LEADER'S TEAM AFTER CREATING THEIR OWN ACCOUNT
   FIRST?

Honest answer: today they can't, not with the account they already made. Teams
live inside one brokerage, so to be on a Team Lead's team a person first has to
be a member of that same brokerage, and you join a brokerage by accepting an
invitation. The invitation only works for a brand-new email. If the person
already created their own account, that email is "taken" by their own
one-person workspace, so the invite is refused.

The clean path today is to be invited first, then create the account from that
invite. The Admin or Team Lead sends the invitation and can point it straight at
the team, so the person lands on the team automatically. After that, the Admin
or the Team Lead can add or move them between teams (only Agents, Coordinators,
and Team Leads can sit on a team).

If someone already made their own account by mistake, the only options right now
are to invite them under a different email, or for me to build a proper "leave
my workspace and join this brokerage" flow. That flow does not exist yet, and it
is the same gap as the self-signup role question. I am flagging it so we can
decide whether to build it.


C. CAN A TRANSACTION COORDINATOR JOIN A BROKERAGE, AND HOW DO THE AGENT AND TC
   CONNECT?

Yes. A TC joins a brokerage exactly like any staff member: an Admin or Team Lead
invites them in. Once in, they are part of the brokerage and can work across its
deals.

On who owns and starts the deal: a transaction can be created by an Agent, Team
Lead, Admin, or the TC, but every deal is "owned" by an agent. There is a
"Whose transaction is it?" picker, so the owner can always be set to the correct
agent even when someone else created it.

There are two ways the agent and TC connect, and both happen inside the same
brokerage:

- If the agent starts the deal: the agent creates it (and owns it), then adds
  the TC to that transaction as the coordinator. The TC gets a notification and
  the deal appears in their queue.

- If the TC starts the deal: the TC creates it and uses the "Whose transaction
  is it?" picker to set the agent as the owner. The agent is now attached to
  their own deal, and the TC keeps working it.

The single requirement either way is that the agent and TC are in the same
brokerage. The thing that links them is the transaction itself, assigning a
person to a deal, rather than a permanent agent-to-TC pairing. So one in-house
TC can be attached to many agents' deals across the brokerage.


One thread runs through all three: today, membership in a brokerage is set when
the account is created (by invitation), and roles are managed afterward by an
Admin. The only real gap is letting somebody who already made their own separate
account fold into an existing brokerage, which is the same decision we have open
on self-signup roles.
