THE 5 STAGES (top to bottom), each a large rounded box:

STAGE 1 - Start the deal
"You begin a new transaction and upload the signed contract, plus any counters
or addenda."

STAGE 2 - The system reads the contract
"It pulls out the key facts for you: the property address, the people on the
deal, the important dates (contract acceptance and closing), and the deal's
details. You confirm anything it could not find."
Small note on this stage: "This is the only place the AI reads the contract.
Everything after this comes from your own task list, not from AI."

STAGE 3 - The system fits your master task list to THIS deal
Show this stage as ONE large box with a short title inside it: "It picks the
right tasks, assigns each one, and sets the due dates:" followed by these 6
plain-language bullet points (these are the decisions the system makes
automatically):
  - Deal type: Buying or Selling, Financed or Cash, or Dual Agency (you
    represent both buyer and seller). It pulls the matching tasks from your
    master list.
  - Deal specifics: Is there an HOA? An inspection? Who orders the title work?
    Is there a home warranty? Each answer adds or drops the related tasks.
  - Dual agency: if you represent both sides, it merges duplicate tasks into
    one and skips tasks that do not apply (for example, no co-op agent welcome
    when there is no co-op agent).
  - State rules: it includes the right closing tasks for the state (attorney
    closing vs title company closing).
  - Due dates: every deadline is counted automatically from either the Contract
    Acceptance date or the Closing date. If a deadline lands on a weekend or
    holiday, it moves to the next business day.
  - Who does it: each task is assigned to the right person (you, the co-op
    agent, the buyer, the seller, the loan officer, or the title company).
Banner across this stage (the most important message, make it stand out):
"Nothing is made up. The system only uses tasks from your brokerage's existing
master task list, and only the ones that fit this deal."

STAGE 4 - You review before anything is created
"You see the full proposed task list, each task with its due date, who it is
for, and why it is included. You can remove tasks, change a date or the
assigned person, and optionally ask the AI to suggest extra tasks that you
approve or ignore."
Small note: "What you see here is exactly what gets created."

STAGE 5 - Approve and create
"You click Approve & Create. The exact list you reviewed becomes the deal's
task list, with deadlines on the calendar and each task assigned to the right
person."
