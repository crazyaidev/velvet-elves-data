3) Pricing and AI cost

I agree with you, let us not set pricing yet. Testing first so we know the real AI cost is the right call. Here is the direction I am leaning, and I would like your opinion.

A simple way to picture it: the AI charges us by how much it reads and writes, a bit like paying by the word. So the cost depends on how much work we hand it.

A couple of things that already help:
- Most of the app does not use AI at all. Task lists, dates, and reminders run on regular code, which is free. The AI is only used for the heavy thinking, like reading a contract or writing an email.
- During testing I would turn on cost tracking, so after a batch of test deals we have a real "AI cost per deal." Then we set pricing with room for profit. This matches what you suggested.

To keep the cost down without hurting quality, what I am thinking is:
- Use a smaller, cheaper AI for the simple jobs (like sorting an incoming email), and the best AI only for the important jobs (reading a contract, writing a client email).
- Avoid paying twice by saving the AI's work and reusing it, instead of asking again.

On your question about our own servers versus an AI service: I would keep using the two AI services we already use, OpenAI and Claude. They give the best quality, and the steps above keep the cost reasonable. Running our own AI would only be cheaper at very large scale, and the quality drops on hard jobs like reading contracts, so I would not go that way now.

Text and voice are separate. The phone and text provider bills those per message and per minute, not the AI.

That is my thinking. Does it sound right to you, or would you like to talk it through?
